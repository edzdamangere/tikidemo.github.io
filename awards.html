Marks of Mana is a cinematic journey into the heart of a sacred female tradition—the art of tatau. For centuries, the marks on a woman's body have told the story of her lineage, her strength, and her service to her community.

From the lush valleys of Samoa to the remote islands of Papua New Guinea and Fiji, director Lisa Taouma captures the resurgence of female practitioners who are reclaiming this ancient craft. Through stunning cinematography and intimate interviews, the film explores how these patterns are not merely ink, but a living connection to the ancestors.

The documentary challenges Western perceptions of "body art," positioning tatau as a profound cultural archive. As we witness the pain and the pride of those receiving and giving these marks, we understand that to wear the tatau is to carry the weight and the mana of an entire history.
